The Architect

I take systems people live inside and never see — their data, the law, scripture — and make them into something a person can hold.

Roots in video and performing arts from 2017. Twenty years before that reading fraud patterns for a living, seeing the structure under ordinary transactions. In 2026 the two met in public. I came back from nothing once, on one small act I didn’t understand at the time. The work since is about handing other people the thing they couldn’t see.

01About the practice

Know Thyself is the thread. Every piece takes a hidden system and gives an ordinary person a way to see it and act.

The practice is not new. In 2015 I wrote Down to Earth, a memoir. From 2017 I worked in video and performing arts, and helped develop a young performer who records today. The analyst years ran alongside — telecoms, insurance, operations — two decades reading the patterns most people never look at twice. It sits in a field that treats data and administrative identity as material for art.

An established voice with a decade behind it. No commission, no funded show, no established partner. Made in the open, on my own time. The rate of output is the discipline.

Currently developing the data-sovereignty arm of the practice through DIG, alongside research into interactive narrative for young audiences.

03Earlier practice · Video, 2017

Before the code, the camera. In 2017 I produced and documented London’s grassroots music scene — convening artists, platforming young voices, recording it as it was. The same instinct runs through the work now: give the unseen a way to be seen.

A note on the footage. These are documentary records of a live street-music scene, unedited and of their moment. They carry strong language and a raw setting. They are shown as the ground the current practice grew from, not as current work.
